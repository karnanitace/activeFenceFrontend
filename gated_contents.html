Introduction

Today’s online world grants child predators a variety of ways to share, interact, and spread pedophilic content. While child sexual abuse material (CSAM) is considered taboo, it is accesible on almost every legitimate online platform, and predators use a variety of methods to spread and consume such content while remaining under the radar .

Becoming familiar with the ways predators use online platforms is the first necessary step to combating the problem. This report features a sample of predator tactics online as identified using ActiveFence’s powerful cross-platform technologies and AI.

The Online Behaviours of Predators

A core aspect of pedophilic behavior is the collection of child pornographic content. This collection is a source of pride for predators, used for personal gratification, as well as trading and distributing within predator communities. The act of trading is both a means of “showing off” and receiving community validation, but can also be a part of an exchange for other images, contact information, tips, or even money.

With a wide variety of free or cheap platforms at their disposal, online predators have honed the craft of lurking in the darkest corners of the internet to obtain, store and share CSAM while avoiding detection.

  • Exploiting Nuanced Polices

    Platforms with no strict policy against sexual content are more likely to be abused by predators. This is particularly common in predators spreading “teen” sexual materials. The vague description of the content may allow CSAM to appear legitimate.

  • Using Internal Slang

    Predators use specific jargon which is only used within the pedophilic community, in order to freely communicate on clearnet social networks. Terms like Nolimits/Nolims, Megalinks, Jailbait/JB can be used to describe CSAM in a way that may not raise any red flags among other users, moderators or AI detection systems without sufficient expertise.
